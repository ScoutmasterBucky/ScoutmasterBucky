World Conservation Award

The World Conservation Award provides an opportunity for individual Wolf Scouts, Bear Scouts, Webelos Scouts, Scouts BSA, Sea Scouts, and Venturers to “think globally” and “act locally” to conserve and improve our environment. This program is designed to make youth members aware that all nations are closely related through natural resources, and that we and our world environment are interdependent.

The emblem is worn centered on the right pocket of the Scout uniform shirt as a temporary patch or may be placed on the back of the merit badge sash.

Upon successful completion, participants earn the Whitewater Rafting patch along with their achievement card. Completion indicates the individual has experienced a valuable introduction to whitewater rafting.

For more information and the award application, go to Scouting America World Conservation Award.

Requirements

A.
Do the following:
1.
Environmental Science OR Sustainability merit badge.
2.
Soil and Water Conservation OR Fish and Wildlife Management merit badge.
3.
Citizenship in the World merit badge.
B.
AND participate in a conservation project as a part of an approved Scouting program totaling at least three hours that addressess a conservation need common to more than one country.
